SWMM5's Groundwater Routine

SWMM5's groundwater module computes lateral flow between a subcatchment's aquifer and the drainage system. It uses a two-zone model: an upper unsaturated zone (tracks moisture content θ) and a lower saturated zone (tracks water table elevation Hgw). The lateral flow equation has three terms that the neural network must discover:

Term 1: GW Outflow
A₁(Hgw − H*)B₁
When Hgw > H*, groundwater flows OUT to the drain. A₁ controls magnitude, B₁ controls nonlinearity. B₁ > 1 means flow accelerates as head rises.
Term 2: SW Inflow
−A₂(Hsw − H*)B₂
Surface water can push flow BACK into the aquifer. This is the flow reversal the network must discover. High Hsw reduces or reverses net GW flow.
Term 3: Coupling
A₃ · Hgw · Hsw
Nonlinear interaction between GW and SW heads. Creates a product term the network must learn — fundamentally different from the power-law terms.
Two-Zone Model
Upper Zone (Unsaturated)
Tracks moisture content θ. Receives infiltration from surface, loses water to ET and percolation downward. dθ/dt = (infil − ET − perc) / dupper. Percolation rate depends on θ and saturated conductivity K.
Lower Zone (Saturated)
Tracks water table elevation Hgw. Receives percolation from upper zone, loses water to lateral GW flow. dHgw/dt = (perc − Qgw) / (porosity). The lateral flow equation governs discharge to drains.
